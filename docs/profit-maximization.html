4.3 Profit Maximization

Assume we have a single output that uses workers. The output price is \(p\) and the wage per worker is \(w\). Then the profit maximization problem can be written as: \[\max \quad p \cdot f(L) - w \cdot L\] And the solution to this problem can be written as: \[p \cdot f'(L) = w \quad \Leftrightarrow \quad f'(L) = \frac{w}{p}\] Put differently \(\pi = p \cdot y - w \cdot x\). Solving for \(y\): \[y =\frac{\pi - w \cdot x}{p} = \frac{\pi}{p} + \frac{w}{p} \cdot x\] The profit maximization problem as a function of output is \(\pi(q) = R(q) - C(q)\) where \(R = p \cdot q\). To find the profit maximizing output, we need the marginal revenue \[\frac{\Delta R}{\Delta q} = p\] and the marginal cost \[\frac{\Delta C}{\Delta Q} = MC\] Hence, the profit maximization condition is: \(MC(q) = MR(q)\). This is true for any market structure. What differs across market structures is marginal revenue.
